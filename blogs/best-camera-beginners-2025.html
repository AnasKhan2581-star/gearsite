Best Camera for Beginners in 2025: The Complete Honest Guide

April 2026 12 min read GearBaskets

Every beginner asks the same question: which camera should I buy? The internet responds with a flood of spec sheets, affiliate links and opinions. We are going to cut through that and give you a straight answer.

This guide covers the three brands beginners actually consider — Sony, Canon and Fujifilm — and tells you exactly which camera to buy depending on your use case and budget.

The Short Answer (Before the Details)

Best beginner camera under ₹80,000 / $850 / €800: Sony ZV-E10 II. Same sensor as cameras costing twice as much. Shoots 4K 10-bit video. Simple enough for day one.

Best beginner camera under ₹1,25,000 / $1,400 / €1,300: Sony a6700. The best APS-C camera available. 4K 120fps, 5-axis IBIS, AI autofocus. Professional results from a beginner-friendly body.

Best beginner camera for photography purists: Canon EOS R50. Dual Pixel CMOS AF is unmatched for reliable autofocus. Great for clothing and product photography.

Best beginner camera for unique visual style: Fujifilm X-S20. Film Simulations create a signature look straight out of camera with zero editing.

Why the Sony a6700 Appears in Most of Our Baskets

The Sony a6700 produces 4K 120fps 10-bit 4:2:2 video. That specification alone justifies its price for anyone serious about video content creation. The 6K-oversampled 4K means images are cleaner than most full-frame cameras costing three times as much.

The 5-axis in-body image stabilisation (IBIS) means you can handhold the camera and still get usable footage without a gimbal. This matters enormously for run-and-gun content creation.

The AI subject recognition tracks faces, eyes, animals and even moving clothing. For fashion creators, this means the camera never loses focus on the outfit even when the model turns.

The Sony ZV-E10 II: The Smart Budget Choice

Here is the fact most YouTube reviews gloss over: the Sony ZV-E10 II uses the exact same 26MP sensor as the a6700. The image quality is identical.

What you give up: no IBIS (you need a gimbal), no EVF (you use the screen), and no 4K 120fps (maximum is 4K 60fps). What you gain: ₹46,000 saved — enough to buy a complete gimbal and storage card.

For most beginners, the ZV-E10 II is the more logical purchase. Put the saved money into glass (lenses) which has a bigger impact on image quality than the camera body.

Canon EOS R50: The Autofocus Champion

Canon's Dual Pixel CMOS AF II is the most reliable autofocus system available in this price range. It locks on and tracks subjects with an accuracy that Sony's AI system does not quite match in challenging conditions.

For creators who shoot clothing on models, this reliability matters. The Canon RF ecosystem also has a wider range of affordable quality lenses than Sony APS-C.

The limitation: 4K video is cropped on the R50, and it only shoots 8-bit. For video-first creators, Sony wins. For photography-first creators, Canon R50 is a strong contender.

Fujifilm X-S20: The Color Science Option

Fujifilm cameras produce images with a distinctive warmth and rendering of skin tones and fabric colors that no amount of post-processing can fully replicate on Sony or Canon footage.

The Film Simulations — Classic Chrome, Velvia, Eterna — give you a consistent visual signature that becomes part of your brand. Fashion and travel creators who want a recognizable aesthetic without hours of editing should seriously consider Fujifilm.

The trade-off: the lens ecosystem costs more, and Fujifilm bodies are typically priced at a premium.

Which Lens to Buy First

This is more important than the camera body choice. Our top recommendation regardless of brand: the Sigma 18-50mm f/2.8. Available in Sony E-mount, Fujifilm X-mount and Canon RF-S mount. The f/2.8 aperture produces professional-looking bokeh for product and portrait work. Silent autofocus motor works perfectly for video. At 290g it is lightweight enough for gimbal work.
